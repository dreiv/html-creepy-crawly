In casual language, people use "bugs" to mean all sorts of small creepy-crawlies: insects, spiders, etc. However, "Bugs" (true bugs) are actually just one order of insects (Hemiptera). This page provides a summary of the main classes or groups of creepy-crawlies.

🐞 Beetles (Coleoptera)

Beetles make up the largest order of insects, with more than 350,000 known species. They are recognized by their hardened forewings (elytra) that cover and protect the delicate hindwings and abdomen.

Many beetles play important ecological roles. Some, like ladybugs, are beneficial predators that feed on crop pests such as aphids. Others recycle nutrients by breaking down animal waste.

🐜 True Bugs (Hemiptera)

True bugs include stink bugs, cicadas, and aphids. They possess distinctive piercing-sucking mouthparts adapted for feeding on plant sap or other insects.

While some are beneficial predators, others are destructive agricultural pests that weaken plants by draining their sap.

🦋 Butterflies & Moths (Lepidoptera)

Butterflies and moths are recognizable by their colorful wings covered in tiny scales. Both undergo complete metamorphosis, transforming from caterpillar to winged adult.

As adults, they are important pollinators, transferring pollen as they sip nectar from flowers. They are also vital food sources for birds and bats.

Image by Didier Descouens - CC BY-SA 4.0

🦟 Flies & Mosquitoes (Diptera)

Flies and mosquitoes belong to the order Diptera, meaning "two wings." They use a single functional pair of wings and have incredible agility in flight.

Despite their reputation, flies are essential decomposers and pollinators in most Earth ecosystems.

Source: Waarneming.nl - CC BY 3.0

🐝 Bees, Wasps, Ants (Hymenoptera)

This group is known for complex behaviors and social structures. Many species live in colonies with distinct roles for workers, queens, and males.

Bees are especially famous for pollination and honey production, while ants are critical soil engineers that recycle nutrients.

Image by Trounce - CC BY-SA 2.5

🕷️ Spiders (Araneae)

Spiders are arachnids, distinguished by eight legs and a lack of antennae. They use silk to build webs, egg sacs, or safety lines.

While often feared, they are highly beneficial predators that help control insect populations worldwide.
